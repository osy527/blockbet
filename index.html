How BlockBet Works

Everything you need to know — mechanics, payouts, safety, and what to watch out for.

What am I predicting?
Every Base block has a block hash — a 64-character hexadecimal string made of digits (0–9) and letters (a–f). You predict whether the very last character of the next block's hash will be a digit or a letter. Base produces a new block roughly every 2 seconds.
How are payouts calculated?
There are no fixed multipliers. Instead, two pools form each round — one for Digit, one for Letter. After the result, the losing pool is entirely distributed to the winners, proportional to how much each winner staked. If you staked 10% of the winning pool, you receive 10% of the combined total. A 1% platform fee is deducted from every bet at entry — not from payouts.
What if I bet after the window closes?
Each round has a target block — bets are only accepted before it is mined. If your transaction is confirmed after the window closes, it is automatically queued for the next round. Your USDC is never lost, it just joins the following bet. This is handled by the smart contract — no action needed from you.
Is the outcome fair and verifiable?
Results come from blockhash() — the cryptographic hash of a future block that nobody can predict or forge. The outcome is fully on-chain and verifiable by anyone on Basescan. For very large prize pools, Chainlink VRF can be integrated as an additional layer of manipulation resistance.
What are the real probabilities?
Each hex character is equally likely to be any of 16 values (0–9 = 10 values, a–f = 6 values). So statistically, digits appear ~62.5% of the time and letters ~37.5%. This means the letter side is less likely to win, but when it does, winners share the larger digit pool — naturally balancing the payout. No artificial odds are applied.
What do I need to participate?
You need a MetaMask wallet with USDC on Base, and a small amount of ETH for gas (typically under $0.01 per transaction). First-time users must approve USDC to the contract once — this is a one-time transaction. After that, betting is a single click.
Who settles each round?
The settleRound() function is public — anyone can call it after the target block is mined. BlockBet operates a bot that automatically settles rounds within seconds. Winners receive USDC directly to their wallet in the same transaction. No manual claiming required.
What should I watch out for?
Only bet amounts you are comfortable losing. Because pools fluctuate each round, your estimated payout changes as more people join. Never share your seed phrase or private key. BlockBet is a decentralized contract — there is no company, no support team, and no refunds. Verify the contract address on Basescan before interacting.
